# Teaching Team

## Maryam Hosseini (Instructor)

### Office Hours: Wednesdays 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM (Wurster, 406a)

<p>Hi everyone! I’m Maryam Hosseini (she/her), and I’m an Assistant Professor in the Department of City &amp; Regional Planning. My research primarily explores how the design of the built environment influences how people use and experience cities, with a focus on pedestrian mobility, urban accessibility, and walkability. I've developed various open-source tools, such as <a href="https://github.com/VIDA-NYU/tile2net" target="_blank">Tile2Net</a>, the first open-source tool for creating geo-referenced pedestrian networks from aerial imagery.</p>
<p>Outside of work, I enjoy painting with watercolors, reading comic books, playing video games, and watching tech YouTube channels. I’m excited to see where your ideas will take you as you work through turning them into actual code and visualizations! Don’t hesitate to reach out if you’d like to chat—whether it’s about the course, your projects, or even ideas beyond the class.</p>
